How It Works

In the background, there is over 100 lines of code (JavaScript) that takes all the letters in the English alphabet and randomly makes them Capitalized. While it's doing that, it also sprinkles in numbers and symbols.

Why Is It All Random?

Well, the most common way for hackers to get a password is called a dictionary attack or brute force attack.

Dictionary And Brute Force Attacks

How these attacks work is through another progamming or coding language, for example Python, C# and Java. The hacker has a list of common passwords that people use and the hacker will compare the passwords to a encrypted password. The program they make will encrypt the list of passwords the same way the password they are looking is. When the progam finds a pair of encrypted passwords that completely match, it will tell the hacker and he or she now has someones password.
